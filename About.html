About Research Teaching

I am an Assistant Professor of Economics in the Faculty of Political Science and Economics at Waseda University, Tokyo, Japan. Previously, I taught at Meiji Gakuin University and held research positions at the Centre for Policy Dialogue (CPD) in Bangladesh, the Overseas Development Institute (ODI) in the UK, and other organizations.

My research interests include labor economics, development economics, microeconomics, and applied economics. I am also passionate about agricultural and public economics, as well as macroeconomics. Currently, I am involved in several projects on migration, labor markets, and intergenerational transmission. I teach public economics, data and econometric methods, development economics, and labor economics.

My academic journey includes a PhD in Development Economics from the National Graduate Institute for Policy Studies (GRIPS), Tokyo, Japan, an MSc in Economics from Queen Mary University of London, UK, and a BSS in Economics from Jagannath University, Bangladesh.

I am proficient in R, Stata, and LaTeX, and occasionally use Python, MATLAB, MS Office, and Eviews for research and teaching.

Beyond academia, I am actively involved in voluntary activities such as mentoring, scholarship guidance, leading student organizations, fundraising, tree plantation, anti-drug and anti-harassment initiatives, blood donation, and community cleaning in Bangladesh, UK, and Japan.

I am passionate about knowledge sharing and voluntarism. I enjoy reading, engaging in discussions, walking, hiking, and exploring places of cultural and historical significance.
